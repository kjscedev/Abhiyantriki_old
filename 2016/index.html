ISRO Exhibition

Indian Space Research Organisation will hold a two day long exhibition on 30th September and 1st October.

  • • The Vikram Sarabhai Space Exhibition (VSSE) will have static panels related to remote sensing satellites, about Chandrayaan-1 and Mars mission, models of camera of mars mission, satellite images and parts used in satellites will be displayed.

  • • Models of launch vehicles like PSLV & GSLV will be put up, various satellite models of INSAT and IRS, OCM, Aryabhatta, RISAT, etc. and a working model showing satellites orbiting around earth will be displayed.

  • • 6 senior ISRO scientists will interact with students and share their views on how the Indian space community evolved over the years.

  • • Video documentaries on launching of various missions, Mars mission and also on how to join ISRO will be shown.

  • •This exhibition will act as an incentive for research papers and development scenarios in the field of space exploration and technology in our country.

SCMU KJSCE OPEN

The SCMU KJSCE Open 2016 was held on 27th and 28th of August in the Vidyavihar Campus. The participants could be seen abuzz with excitement and anticipation. The competition received an overwhelming support from all quarters. The participating demographic varied from school children to post graduates, more than a 100 students participated in the event. It was the first pre-event to kickstart Abhiyantriki 2016. The event was hosted by the Speed Cubing Mumbai Unlimited (SCMU) in association with the World Cube Association. The competition had a total of 12 novel challenges which included 2x2, 3x3, Clock, 6x6, 7x7, Pyraminx, 3x3 Blindfold (BLD), 3x3 One-handed (OH), Multiple Blindfolded, Square-1, Skewb and Fewest Moves challenge. Two of the participants broke a national record for two different events in the competition. Aniket Das broke the national record of Pyraminx average, with an average of 2.93 seconds. Also, Shivam Bansal a rubik’s cube veteran, broke the national record in multiple blindfolded cubing by solving 26 out of 31 cubes in 60 minutes time frame blindfolded. It was a great experience for all the participants with budding minds wishing to venture into speedcubing.

About KJSCE

Set in the heart of the city, K. J. Somaiya College of Engineering is located in the Somaiya campus at Vidyavihar. Catering to about 40,000 students every day, this campus offers a multidisciplinary education over diverse fields of Engineering, Management, Commerce, Arts and Sciences. Along with numerous institutes, Somaiya Vidyavihar boasts a campus covering 65 acres. Along with being granted Autonomous status from the academic year 2014-15, K. J. Somaiya College of Engineering also enjoys the status of being graded ‘A’ by the NBA (National Board of Accreditation). Being the first Self financed college to receive autonomy status, KJSCE still remains affiliated to Mumbai University.

About Abhiyantriki

Borrowed from sanskrit, “Abhiyantriki” stands for Engineering, which is exactly what Abhiyantriki aims to celebrate. It hosts a variety of engineering disciplines and mindsets, with the purpose of putting knowledge and innovation on the platform it needs and deserves. Being celebrated for two days for the first time, Abhiyantriki now boasts an extended lineup of events and competitions. It is finally the time for Abhiyantriki to step out of its own shadow and reach for the stars..
